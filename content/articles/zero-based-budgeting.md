Zero-based budgeting (ZBB) is a method of budgeting that starts from scratch with every new budgeting period, typically a year or month. Every expense, no matter how large or small, needs to be justified and approved. This is different from traditional budgeting, which often builds on historical spending levels, simply adjusting them slightly for the new period.

How it works:

- Every expense gets reviewed: Each budget line item, from rent to office supplies, gets a fresh look. Justification for each expense is required, demonstrating its value and contribution to overall goals.

- Prioritization: Expenses are then prioritized based on their importance and how well they align with the budget's overall goals. Less essential expenses may be reduced or eliminated entirely.

- Allocation: Resources are then allocated to the highest-priority items, ensuring every dollar has a clear purpose and contributes to the desired outcomes.

Key Benefits

- Cost savings: By forcing justification for every expense, ZBB can identify and eliminate waste and inefficiencies, leading to cost savings.

- Improved efficiency: Prioritizing resources on the most impactful activities leads to a more efficient allocation of resources.

- Increased accountability: The justification process promotes greater accountability for financial decisions.

- Enhanced budgeting skills: ZBB encourages a deeper understanding of the budget and how it aligns with organizational goals.

Drawbacks:

- Time-consuming: Implementing and maintaining ZBB can be time-consuming and require significant effort from all levels of an organization.

- Resistance to change: Shifting from traditional budgeting methods to ZBB can face resistance from those used to the old system.

- Data and analysis requirements: ZBB relies on accurate data and thorough analysis, which can be challenging to gather and maintain.
